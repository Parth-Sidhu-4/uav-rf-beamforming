# PART I — PAKISTAN / پاکستان

## ENGLISH VERSION

### Important Notice for Users in the Islamic Republic of Pakistan

This repository contains simulation code and research relating to **adaptive
beamforming, electronic warfare (EW) signal processing, jamming suppression,
and unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) communications resilience**. These
technologies are dual-use in nature and are subject to the following laws of
the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.

#### 1. Export Control Act 2004
#### برآمد کنٹرول ایکٹ 2004

The **Export Control on Goods, Technologies, Material and Equipment Related
to Nuclear and Biological Weapons and their Delivery Systems Act, 2004**
("Export Control Act") is Pakistan's primary legislation governing the
transfer of strategic technologies. The Act is administered by the
**Strategic Export Controls Division (SECD)** under the Ministry of Foreign
Affairs, Islamabad.

Section 3 of the Export Control Act prohibits the export, re-export, or
transfer of controlled goods, technologies, and software without prior
authorisation from the competent authority. Phased array antenna systems,
electronic warfare signal processing algorithms, and AI-driven RF systems
used in military UAV applications may fall within the scope of Pakistan's
**Strategic Goods List (SGL)**, which mirrors international control lists
including the Wassenaar Arrangement Dual-Use List.

Pakistani persons and entities accessing or using this repository must
independently assess whether their use requires prior authorisation from
the **SECD** or any other competent authority.

Contact: Strategic Export Controls Division (SECD)
Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Constitution Avenue, Islamabad
Website: https://www.mofa.gov.pk

#### 2. Pakistan Penal Code (PPC) — National Security Provisions

Sections 123-A and 124 of the **Pakistan Penal Code (PPC)** prohibit
activities that are deemed prejudicial to the safety or interests of
Pakistan. Section 123-A specifically prohibits advocating or advising
actions that may be detrimental to Pakistan's sovereignty and territorial
integrity. While academic simulation research does not fall within the
ordinary scope of these provisions, Pakistani users engaging with
EW-adjacent technologies should exercise appropriate caution.

#### 3. Official Secrets Act 1923 (as applicable in Pakistan)

Pakistan continues to apply the **Official Secrets Act 1923** (inherited
from British India), which prohibits the collection, communication, or
publication of information that could be useful to an adversary. Pakistani
users who are associated with or employed by the Pakistan Armed Forces,
Inter-Services Intelligence (ISI), or any defence-related public body must
ensure that accessing or using this repository is consistent with their
obligations under this Act and their applicable security clearance terms.

#### 4. Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016 (PECA) & 2025 Amendment

The **Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act, 2016** (PECA), as significantly 
amended by the **Prevention of Electronic Crimes (Amendment) Act 2025**, 
regulates cyber activities in Pakistan under the oversight of the **Digital 
Rights Protection Authority (DRPA)**. Section 10 of PECA prohibits unauthorised access
to information systems in ways that could prejudice national security.
This repository is a public GitHub repository; accessing it does not
constitute an unauthorised act. However, any Pakistani user who uses
algorithms from this repository to develop a system subsequently used
against Pakistan's national interest may face liability under the amended PECA.

# PART II — BANGLADESH / বাংলাদেশ

## ENGLISH VERSION

### Important Notice for Users in the People's Republic of Bangladesh

This repository contains simulation code and research relating to **adaptive
beamforming, electronic warfare (EW) signal processing, and UAV communications
resilience**. Users in Bangladesh should be aware of the following applicable
legal frameworks before accessing or using this repository.

#### 1. Export Policy Order and Import Policy Order

Bangladesh regulates the import and export of strategic goods through
successive **Export Policy Orders** and **Import Policy Orders** issued under
the Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1950. While Bangladesh does not yet
have a dedicated, comprehensive dual-use export control law equivalent to the
Wassenaar Arrangement, the Import Policy Order restricts the importation of
goods and technologies related to defence and national security.

Bangladeshi users accessing this repository should assess whether downloading
or using EW-related simulation code constitutes importation of a controlled
technology under the current Import Policy Order, particularly if the user is
affiliated with the Bangladesh Armed Forces, Directorate General of Forces
Intelligence (DGFI), or any defence-related government body.

#### 2. Cyber Security Act & 2026 AI Amendments
#### সাইবার নিরাপত্তা আইন এবং ২০২৬ এর সংশোধনী

The Digital Security Act 2018 was replaced by the **Cyber Security Act 2023**, 
which was subsequently updated by the **Cyber Security (Amendment) Bill, 2026**. 
The 2026 amendment specifically empowers authorities regarding AI-generated 
content and grants the BTRC increased authority to block digital content that 
threatens the sovereignty, integrity, or security of Bangladesh.

While accessing a public academic repository does not ordinarily constitute
an offence, Bangladeshi users who subsequently use the AI algorithms 
(such as the FFN surrogate model) from this repository in ways that violate 
the 2026 Act must exercise appropriate caution.

#### 3. Official Secrets Act 1923 (as applicable in Bangladesh)

Bangladesh, like Pakistan, applies the **Official Secrets Act 1923**
(inherited from British India and retained post-independence). Personnel
associated with the Bangladesh Armed Forces, Border Guard Bangladesh (BGB),
Rapid Action Battalion (RAB), or any intelligence agency must ensure that
their access to and use of this repository is consistent with their obligations
under this Act and their applicable security clearance terms.

#### 4. Customs Act 1969 and Strategic Technology Controls

Under the **Customs Act 1969**, the importation of goods and technologies
that could be used for military purposes may require prior clearance. Although
software code downloaded from a public platform is not ordinarily treated as
an imported physical good, Bangladeshi authorities retain broad discretion
in interpreting import restrictions in matters touching national security.
